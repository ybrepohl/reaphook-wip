About

REAPHOOK is a revisionist story of two well-known characters and how their infamous relationship began.

This short film written and directed by Kelsey Pigden will be submitted as a thesis film for second year of Film Production at Confederation College in Thunder Bay, Ontario, and premiered at the annual Conflix Film Night. It will also be shown in the Niagara Region.

Cast

  • Daniel Pianezza

    (James Miller)

    Daniel began his screen career in grade school doing short films which led to acting in his own movies in high school. As a film production student at Confederation, he managed to appear in several short films between creating his own. Daniel is determined to embrace the challenging role of James.

  • Terri Lynn Fucile

    (Beatrice Miller)

    Terri Lynn is no stranger to Conflix Productions, having appeared in numerous short films over the past 10 years.

  • Tesla Shaye

    (Sam)

    Tesla is a veteran of both stage and screen. Her experiences in short films, little theatre productions, and the Sears Drama Competition have convinced her to pursue a career in acting, preferably on Broadway. Tesla is bringing her comedic drama talents to the role of Sam.

  • Kelsey Pigden

    (Lenore)

    Kelsey began her acting career in grade school doing musicals. Trained at the Niagara Falls School of Drama and having studied with Frank Rossi, Kelsey has enjoyed a wide array of roles over the years. She is excited to undertake the part of the wild child, Lenore.

  • Maya Groulx

    (Gwendolyn Miller)

    Maya discovered a talent for acting in elementary school musicals. Combined with dance training, this led to a role in Garden City's staging of "Annie". Maya is currently studying drama at the Sanctuary Centre for the Arts. OSP is thrilled to be introducing this young actress to the film world in the pivotal role of Gwendy.

  • Alexandra Rheaume

    (Pan)

    Alexandra started in drama in high school mostly as part of the set design and stage crews but discovered a real love of acting in college. She has previously appeared in Conflix shorts, notably "Wishful Thinking", and is delighted to tackle the role of Pan.
